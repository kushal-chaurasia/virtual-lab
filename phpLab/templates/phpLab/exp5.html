Do While Loop

Theory

1.1 Introduction
The while and for loop constructs tests the termination condition at the top. However the do..while construct tests the termination condition at the bottom after passing through all the statements provided in the body of the loop.
The syntax for the do... while is
do
{
one or more statements to be executed;
} while(test_condition);

Here in this construct first the statement(s) is/are executed and then condition is checked. If the condition is true(non-zero) the statements inside the loop are executed again otherwise not. As condition is checked at the end of the iteration, this loop is also called as exit-controlled loop. Thus the body of the loop is always executed at least once.
